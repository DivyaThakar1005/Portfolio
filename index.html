About Me

"I turn complex, messy data into clear stories that help businesses make smarter decisions — from the first SQL query to the final stakeholder dashboard."

I'm Divya Thakar, a Data Analytics graduate student at San Jose State University (expected May 2026), based in San Jose, CA. My work sits at the intersection of data analytics, business intelligence, and statistical modeling — transforming raw datasets into insights that drive real business outcomes.

My analytics work spans exploratory data analysis in Python (Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib), building interactive dashboards in Tableau and Power BI, writing complex SQL for business reporting, and applying forecasting models to real-world datasets. I've built KPI dashboards for an edtech platform at PlatoPlus, published research on data pipeline optimization, and conducted climate-agriculture analysis achieving 90% forecast accuracy.

I care about communicating data clearly — whether that means crafting a clean visualization for a C-suite audience, structuring a statistical model to answer a specific business question, or optimizing a query so a dashboard loads in real time. I work across the full analytics workflow: data cleaning, EDA, modeling, visualization, and insight delivery.

Outside of analytics, I enjoy research at the intersection of AI and data systems. I've co-authored 6 conference publications including IEEE SNPD 2025 and IEEE CAI 2026. I'm open to relocate and excited about opportunities in data analytics, business analytics, BI engineering, and data science.
